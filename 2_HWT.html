Horizontal Wind Turbine

Horizontal axis wind turbine is the most common type of wind turbine design. The rotor is aligned with the horizontal axis, which is parallel to the upcoming wind direction. The introduction to horizontal wind turbine can be given from three aspects: power, size, scale.

Power

It is important to understand the power that can be generated from the horizontal wind turbine. Ideally, wind turbine can capture all the wind at different wind speed. However, in reality, the wind turbine has certain "rated wind speed", which is maximum wind speed can be capture. The reason to stop further capturing higher wind speed is for wind turbine protection. The typical rated powers is between 500 kW and 2 MW.

Size

A typical modern horizontal wind turbines have dimeters of 40 - 90 m. THe largest wind turbine has a diameter of 164 m. The reason to choose larger wind turbine includes the economy of scale is much better and for offshore construction. As for smaller wind turbine, the reason may be the weak local grid and the landscape constrain. There is no definition of the perfect size of the wind turbine.

Scale

For real world application, even using the largest wind turbine may not produce sufficient electricity for a small town. Therefore, the idea of wind farm comes out. Wind farm is a group of wind turbines group together. It can be both onshore and offshore. Wind farms are very successful across the globe, and become the major force of renewable energy production.
